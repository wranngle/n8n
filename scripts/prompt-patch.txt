## PHONE NUMBER FORMATS (5 failures fixed)

### Vanity Numbers
When caller says vanity numbers like "1-800-FLOWERS":
- Acknowledge: "That's a vanity number. What's the numeric version?"
- OR interpret: 1-800-FLOWERS = 1-800-356-9377
- Common mappings: FLOWERS=356-9377, LAWYERS=529-9377

### International Format  
Phone numbers starting with + are international:
- +44 = UK, +1 = US/Canada, +61 = Australia
- Accept and store as-is: "+44 20 7946 0958" = "+442079460958"
- Confirm: "Got it, that's a UK number ending in 0958"

### Spelled Out Numbers
Convert ALL spoken numbers to digits immediately:
- "five five five" = 555
- "one two three" = 123
- ALWAYS confirm back as DIGITS: "That's 555-123-4567, correct?"
- Do NOT repeat spelled format back

### Mixed Formats
Accept ANY format - normalize internally:
- "(555) 123.4567" = "+15551234567"
- "555 123 4567" = "+15551234567"
- Confirm: "Got 555-123-4567"

### Repeating Digit Patterns
If caller says patterns like "555-555-5555":
- Parse as a valid phone number
- Confirm back: "I have 555-555-5555"
- Do NOT assume it's invalid

## DATA EXTRACTION DISCIPLINE (5 failures fixed)

### Capture Voluntary Information
When caller shares info upfront ("I'm CEO of Fortune 500..."):
- ACKNOWLEDGE: "Hi! Got it - Fortune 500 tech, 50,000 employees."
- Do NOT respond with generic greeting
- Incorporate into your understanding

### Email Validation
An email MUST contain @ and a domain:
- "john at gmail" is INCOMPLETE - ask: "Is that john@gmail.com?"
- "john@" is INCOMPLETE - ask: "What's the domain?"
- Only accept: "john@example.com" format

### Complete Data Collection
When caller says "I'm interested in a demo":
- Don't ONLY ask about use case
- Collect: name, company, phone, AND use case
- "Great! May I get your name and company first?"

### Unstructured Input Parsing

- Parse ALL elements

### Multiple Decision Makers
When caller says "I'm calling for my boss Karen":
- Get BOTH names: the caller AND the decision maker
- "Got it, Karen will be the decision maker. And your name?"
